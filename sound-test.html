Magic Wand Chime — 6 Approaches

Move your mouse slowly across each bar to hear the difference

Approach 1
Bell Acoustics — Inharmonic Partials
Real glockenspiel / mark tree physics. Each ping = 3 sine partials at struck-bar ratios (1 : 2.756 : 5.404). Fundamentals 1900–3600 Hz. Sharp attack, exponential bell tail (180–400ms). No pitch bend.
Approach 2
FM Synthesis — Carrier + Modulator
Chowning (1973) bell technique. Modulator drives carrier frequency at a non-integer 1:2.756 ratio — creates glassy spectral sidebands. Modulation index decays 50–80ms: "ping then dissolve to air." Carriers 3000–6500 Hz.
Approach 3
Filtered Noise — Crystal / Glass
White noise through narrow bandpass filters (Q 35–80). No oscillators — each sparkle is resonated noise. Airy and breathy, like crystal or glass catching light. 7–10 particles, 50–130ms decay, 3500–10000 Hz.
Approach 4
Psychoacoustics — Tinkerbell Method
Pitches locked to C-major pentatonic (shifted to sparkle band) so any cluster sounds consonant. Inharmonic partials + 3ms noise transient at attack onset (physical strike cue). Shimmer tail via 28ms feedback delay. Most "fairy" of the four.
Approach 4B
Tinkerbell — Long Tail & Deep Reverb
Same pentatonic psychoacoustic design as #4 but with heavier reverb: feedback raised to 0.88 (≈14s tail), wet level doubled, and a second low-frequency shimmer layer added. The sound decays into a longer, more cavernous shimmer cloud.
Approach 6
Airy Cathedral — Triple Reverb Hall
Single isolated ping (no clusters) with three reverb buses at different delay times: short (35ms), mid (95ms), long (180ms). Each bus creates a distinct echo layer — together they build a cathedral hall. Feedback 0.90 (≈18s tail). Twinkles fire ~150ms apart — slow, deliberate, airy.
Approach 7
Lonely Starfield — Maximum Reverb, Ultra Sparse
One ping at a time, firing ~220ms apart — enough silence between each to let the previous tail breathe. Primary reverb feedback at 0.93 (≈20s+ tail) with 60% wet mix. A warmer sub-shimmer bus adds low-end body to the decay cloud. The reverb from one strike hasn't finished before the next begins.
